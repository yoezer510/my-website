The National Flag of Bhutan

The national flag of Bhutan is divided diagonally into two parts: yellow and orange. The yellow half represents the civil tradition and authority of the King, while the orange symbolizes spiritual traditions, especially Buddhism.

At the center is the white Druk (Thunder Dragon), symbolizing purity and the protection of Bhutan. The dragon holds jewels in its claws, representing the country‚Äôs wealth and security.
