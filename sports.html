Sports of Bhutan

Sports play an important role in Bhutanese life, combining traditional activities with modern games. The most popular and deeply rooted sport in Bhutan is Archery, the national sport.

Archery

Archery is Bhutanâ€™s national sport, played during festivals and gatherings. Unlike Olympic archery, targets are placed much farther away, and matches include singing, dancing, and festivities.

Khuru

Khuru is a traditional dart game, often played during local festivals. Heavy wooden darts are thrown at a small target placed at a distance, making it a fun and competitive social sport.

Digor

Digor are traditional Bhutanese throwing games, similar to shot put. They are played with stones and involve accuracy, strength, and teamwork.

Soksom

A traditional game similar to javelin, where participants throw a long wooden stick at a target from a distance.

Keshey(Traditional Wrestling)

Keshey is a traditional Bhutanese form of wrestling that tests strength, balance, and skill. Practiced mainly in villages and during local festivals, it is both a sport and a social event, bringing communities together to celebrate athleticism and cultural heritage.

Modern Sports

Modern sports like football, basketball, and cricket are gaining popularity, especially among the youth. Bhutan also participates in international competitions, promoting both tradition and modern athletic spirit.
